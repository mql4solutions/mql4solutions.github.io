Keep It Simple

The KISS principle states that most systems work best if they are kept simple rather than made complicated — Keep It Short and Simple

YAGNI is a principle that states not to add functionality until deemed necessary — You Aren't Gonna Need It

Our most successful clients make increments that gradually add combinable abilities or rules only as required after reflection on each increment.

Disappointment and wastage occurs when you specify something too large. When you get hands-on, you realize that only 20% of the features turn out to be successful in practice, and that the other 80% should have taken a different direction.
