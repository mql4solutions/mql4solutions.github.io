Point vs Pip

A Point is the smallest price change for a particular chart given by your particular broker.

For example, 1 Point on EURUSD is 0.00001 if your broker provides 5 digits for EURUSD.

If your broker provides EURUSD with 4 digits, then 1 Point on EURUSD is 0.0001.

You should always use units of Points in defining your robot, because it's the smallest unit and therefore gives you the most flexibility. The meaning of Point is understood on any chart and any broker. Just look at how many digits a chart has, and that's what your robot will use as a Point in its algorithm.

A Pip, to us, is a financial convention used in News and is a bit vague for development. In News and finance, when a person speaks of a pip on EURUSD, they will probably be referring to a price movement of 0.0001.

But in creating a robot with inputs, you want to give it maximum flexibility by defining its units in Points, which specifically means in MT4 to be the smallest given price unit for that chart and that broker you're currently running the robot on.
